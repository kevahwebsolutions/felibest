Frequently Asked Questions
From Installations, emergency plumbing services to slab leak repair, we’ve put together
a comprehensive run down of questions we hear most often.
What do you charge for services?
Our service charges vary depending on the type and complexity of the plumbing job. We provide transparent pricing and will give you a detailed quote before any work begins, ensuring there are no surprises.
Can you install energy-efficient plumbing fixtures?
Absolutely! We can help you choose and install energy-efficient plumbing fixtures, such as low-flow toilets, faucets, and showerheads. These fixtures not only help conserve water but can also reduce your utility bills.
How long will a water heater last?
The lifespan of a water heater typically ranges from 4 to 6 years, depending on the type and maintenance. Regular maintenance can help extend the life of your water heater.
What are the signs that I need to replace my plumbing pipes?
Common signs that your plumbing pipes need replacing include frequent leaks, discolored water, reduced water pressure, and unusual noises like banging or clanging. If you notice any of these signs, it may be time to consider repiping.
What should I do in case of a plumbing emergency?
In the event of a plumbing emergency, such as a burst pipe or severe leak, shut off the main water supply to your home and contact us immediately. We offer 24/7 emergency plumbing services and will respond promptly to minimize damage.
How often should I have my plumbing inspected?
It’s recommended to have your plumbing system inspected at least once a year. Regular inspections can help identify potential issues early, prevent major repairs, and ensure that your plumbing system is functioning properly.
What can I do to prevent clogs in my drains?
To prevent clogs, avoid putting grease, coffee grounds, and food scraps down the kitchen sink. Use a drain screen to catch hair and other debris in the bathroom, and be mindful of what you flush down the toilet. Regular maintenance, such as flushing drains with hot water or a mild drain cleaner, can also help prevent buildup.
What should I do if my water bill is unusually high?
An unusually high water bill could indicate a leak or other plumbing issue. Check for visible leaks around your home and monitor your water meter for any irregularities. If you cannot identify the cause, contact us to perform a thorough inspection and identify any hidden leaks or problems.
